NeighborNet: Bridging the Generative Gap
🏘️ Project Overview
NeighborNet is a hyper-local community web application designed to reconstruct the "social fabric" of modern residential buildings. In an era where neighbors often remain strangers, this platform serves as a digital bridge, fostering communication, mutual aid, and safety.

The application features a unique Dual-Dashboard System—one high-energy interface for younger, tech-savvy residents and a "Silver" minimalist interface for senior citizens—ensuring that technology becomes a tool for inclusion rather than a barrier.

🌟 Key Features
1. The Dual-Dashboard Experience
The Modern Hub (Youngsters/General): A feature-rich environment focused on social networking, building forums, resource sharing (skill-swapping), and event management.

The Silver Hub (Seniors): A hyper-simplified, high-contrast interface designed for accessibility. It features large buttons, zero nested menus, and voice-assisted navigation to eliminate "tech-anxiety."

2. The Community Credit System
A gamified "Karma" economy where residents earn Neighborhood Credits by fulfilling help requests (e.g., carrying groceries, fixing a router, or teaching a skill). These credits build a reputation profile, identifying the most helpful members of the community.

3. Integrated Safety & Emergency Net
One-Touch SOS: A persistent emergency trigger that alerts building security and the five nearest neighbors simultaneously.

Call a Neighbor: A specialized module for non-medical emergencies where immediate physical presence is needed.

Smart Directory: A localized database of the nearest hospitals, pharmacies, and police stations based on the building's specific coordinates.

4. Trilingual Support
To cater to diverse urban demographics, the entire platform is natively available in:

English

Hindi (हिंदी)

Marathi (मराठी)

🛠️ Design Philosophy
Accessibility First
The Senior Dashboard follows WCAG 2.1 guidelines, ensuring that typography is large (minimum 20px), contrast is high, and interactive elements are spaced to prevent accidental clicks.

Privacy & Security
Geo-Fencing: Users are verified via apartment number and building-specific invite codes.

Data Privacy: Neighbors can communicate without sharing private mobile numbers until they choose to do so.

Social Resilience
The project aims to create "First Responders" within the floor itself. By connecting those who have time and energy (youngsters) with those who have wisdom but may need physical assistance (seniors), we create a self-sustaining ecosystem of care.

🚀 Future Roadmap
IoT Integration: Smart-bell alerts for seniors that notify neighbors if a door remains open or an alarm triggers.

Society Voting: Digital ballots for building committee decisions.

Bulk Buying: A module for neighbors to group together for grocery discounts.
